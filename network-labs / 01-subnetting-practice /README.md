# 01 - Subnetting Practice

## What is this?
This folder contains my subnetting exercises and practice problems.
Subnetting is the skill of dividing a big network into smaller networks.
Think of it like dividing a big building into separate rooms — each room has its own address range.

## What I Practiced
- Finding the network address, broadcast address, and usable IP range of any subnet
- Converting between slash notation (/24, /27, /30) and subnet masks (255.255.255.0)
- Dividing a network into multiple smaller subnets
- Solving subnetting problems under 2 minutes without a calculator

## Tools I Used
- Paper and pen (manual practice)
- subnettingpractice.com (daily drills)
- Binary conversion practice

## What I Learned
- How IP addresses are structured in binary
- Why subnetting exists and how it saves IP addresses
- How to calculate network size, usable hosts, and broadcast address
- How to identify which subnet a device belongs to

- Week 2: Solving problems without looking at notes
- Week 3: Solving problems under 5 minutes
- Week 4: Solving problems under 2 minutes
